STEWARDSHIP

Discipleship lesson.

View Lesson
SERMON OUTLINE: STEWARDSHIP OF TIME, TALENTS, AND RESOURCES Theme: Faithful Stewards or Unfaithful Tenants? Main Texts: Matthew 25:14-30. Matthew 21:33-46. Key Verse: 1 Corinthians 4:2. "Moreover it is required in stewards, that a man be found faithful." INTRODUCTION. God entrusts people with responsibilities, opportunities, gifts, resources, and His work. Throughout Scripture, God blesses faithful stewards and judges unfaithful ones. In the Parable of the Talents, faithful servants were rewarded for proper stewardship. In the Parable of the Wicked Tenants, unfaithful tenants abused their position, mistreated the master's servants, and even killed his son. Their failure was not merely poor management; it was rebellion against the owner. The question for every believer is: Am I a faithful steward or an unfaithful tenant? I. THE LESSON OF THE WICKED TENANTS Scripture Matthew 21:33-46 What the Tenants Did. • The landowner planted a vineyard. • He entrusted it to tenants. • When harvest time came, he sent servants to collect his fruit. • The tenants beat one servant. • They killed another. • They stoned another. • Finally, they killed the owner's son. Spiritual Meaning. • The landowner represents God. • The vineyard represents God's Kingdom and possessions. • The tenants represent those entrusted with God's work. • The servants represent God's messengers and prophets. • The son represents Jesus Christ. Lessons. 1. Stewardship Never Removes Ownership The tenants forgot that the vineyard belonged to the owner. Lesson: What God has entrusted to us still belongs to Him. 2. Unfaithful Stewards Become Rebellious The tenants acted as owners instead of caretakers. Lesson: Pride causes people to forget God's authority. 3. God Expects Fruitfulness The owner came looking for fruit. Lesson: God expects results from every gift, resource, and opportunity He gives. 4. Rejection of God's Messengers Leads to Judgment The tenants rejected every servant sent to them. Lesson: How we respond to God's Word and His messengers matters. Conclusion The vineyard was taken away from the wicked tenants and given to others who would produce fruit. II. THE DANGER OF UNFAITHFUL MESSENGERS. Scripture. Proverbs 25:19. "Confidence in an unfaithful man in time of trouble is like a broken tooth, and a foot out of joint." Another Supporting Scripture Proverbs 13:17. "A wicked messenger falleth into mischief: but a faithful ambassador is health." Lessons. • God values faithfulness. • Unfaithfulness causes damage. • An unreliable servant cannot be trusted with important assignments. • Faithful messengers bring healing, solutions, and blessings. Illustration. Trying to walk on a lame foot is painful and ineffective. Likewise, depending on an unfaithful person brings disappointment and loss. Application God wants believers who can be trusted with His work, His Word, His resources, and His people. Conclusion Faithfulness is the foundation of effective stewardship. III. STEWARDSHIP OF TIME. Scripture. Ephesians 5:15-16. Key Points. • Time is God's gift. • Lost time cannot be recovered. • Every day should glorify God. • Wise people redeem the time. Conclusion. A faithful steward values every moment. IV. STEWARDSHIP OF TALENTS. 1 Petro 4:10. Scripture. 1 Peter 4:10 Key Points. • Everyone has a gift. • Gifts must be developed. • Gifts must serve God's purposes. • Gifts should produce fruit. Conclusion. God expects multiplication, not stagnation. The greatest tragedy is not lacking talent but failing to use what God has given. V. STEWARDSHIP OF FINANCES AND RESOURCES Scripture Luke 16:10 Key Points • Money is a stewardship trust. • Faithfulness in little leads to greater responsibility. • Giving honours God. • Wastefulness dishonours stewardship. Conclusion. God rewards wise management. 5. STEWARDSHIP OF OPPORTUNITIES. God places opportunities before us to serve, witness, learn, and grow. Scripture: Galatians 6:10. "As we have opportunity, let us do good to all people." Examples. • Opportunities to serve. • Opportunities to evangelize. • Opportunities to help others. • Opportunities for growth. Application. Missed opportunities often become regrets. Conclusion. Wise stewards recognize and seize God-given opportunities. VI. THE REWARD OF FAITHFUL STEWARDSHIP. Scripture. Matthew 25:21. "Well done, good and faithful servant." Rewards. • Divine favour. • Greater responsibility. • Kingdom influence. • Spiritual growth. • Eternal rewards. Conclusion. God rewards faithfulness, not merely ability. FINAL CONCLUSION. The wicked tenants lost their vineyard because they forgot who the owner was. The unfaithful messenger became like a broken tooth and a lame foot because he could not be trusted. God is looking for faithful men and women who will manage His time, talents, resources, opportunities, and assignments with integrity. May we never become like the wicked tenants who abused their trust, but rather like faithful servants who hear the Master say: "Well done, good and faithful servant." PROPHETIC DECLARATIONS 1. I shall be a faithful steward in God's Kingdom. 2. I reject the spirit of unfaithfulness and rebellion. 3. My life shall produce fruit for God's glory. 4. Every gift God has given me shall flourish. 5. I receive wisdom to manage my time effectively. 6. I receive wisdom to manage my resources faithfully. 7. I shall not waste divine opportunities. 8. My hands shall prosper in every good work. 9. I shall be trustworthy before God and men. 10. I shall not be a broken tooth or a lame foot in God's Kingdom. 11. I shall be a faithful messenger of the Gospel. 12. Divine favour shall rest upon my life. 13. Every seed I sow shall produce a harvest. 14. I shall walk in fruitfulness and increase. 15. My family shall serve the Lord faithfully. 16. I shall finish my assignment successfully. 17. I shall hear the voice of the Master saying, "Well done." 18. Every area of unfaithfulness in my life is being corrected by God's grace. 19. The Lord shall establish me as a faithful steward. 20. In Jesus' mighty Name, I shall bear lasting fruit for the Kingdom of God. Amen.
